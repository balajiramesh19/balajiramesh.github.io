First Hop

Days @ JPMC

When I first started my training at Infosys Mysore, I was excited to learn Python and Java. During my generic training in Python, I thought I had the language mastered until one day, I accidentally wrote a program that kept repeating itself infinitely. I didn't realize my mistake until my computer began to emit smoke and eventually caught fire! Fortunately, the fire was quickly put out and nobody was hurt, but I learned a valuable lesson about the importance of properly testing and debugging code.
On my first day, I was introduced to my new team and given a quick rundown of what I'd be working on. It was all pretty straightforward until my manager asked me if I knew what "corporate technology" was. I was a bit taken aback by the question and could feel the sweat starting to bead on my forehead.
As the days went on, I started to get the hang of things, but I still had a lot to learn. One day, during a team meeting, my manager asked me to explain a particularly tricky concept we had been discussing. Feeling confident, I launched into my explanation, only to realize halfway through that I had no idea what I was talking about. I stumbled over my words and finally just said, "You know what, let me get back to you on that one." My colleagues all looked at me with amusement, and I couldn't help but laugh at myself.
Despite the initial challenges, I soon settled into my new role and learned a lot along the way. And looking back, those first few weeks at JP Morgan will always hold a special place in my heart - after all, there's nothing quite like the experience of diving into a new role headfirst and learning as you go!

Role Full Stack Developer
Designation Software Engineer
LOB Corporate Technology

Worked on the project which was building a platform for the generation of FED mandated and the internal JPMC reports.

The latest technologies of big data and streaming applications were used for the consumption of data from the data reservoir and the query generation of the persisted data in the spark query engine.
