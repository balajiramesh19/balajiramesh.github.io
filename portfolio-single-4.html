Days at Goldman Sachs

When I first started my career at Goldman Sachs as a consultant straight out of college, I was both excited and terrified. I had heard all about the legendary code reviews at the company and was worried that my coding skills weren't up to par. Plus, I had heard that many of the managers were former IIT graduates who could be pretty intimidating.

During my first code review, I nervously presented my code to my manager, who proceeded to point out a slew of errors and bugs. I was devastated and felt like I didn't belong at the company. But then, to my surprise, my manager offered to sit down with me and walk me through the process of fixing my mistakes. I learned so much from him during that session and he quickly became one of my closest mentors and friends at the company.

As I worked on more and more projects, I discovered that the IIT graduates weren't so scary after all. In fact, they were some of the most helpful and supportive colleagues I had ever worked with. They always had a new coding trick up their sleeve and were happy to share their knowledge with me.

Of course, there were still some tough moments, like when I accidentally deleted an entire database during a project (oops!). But even then, my colleagues rallied around me and helped me fix the issue.

Looking back on my time at Goldman Sachs, I realize that it wasn't just about writing code, but also about building relationships and learning from the people around me. And who knows, maybe someday I'll even have the chance to be a scary-but-inspiring mentor to a new crop of fresh-faced college graduates!
